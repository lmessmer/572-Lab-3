WPA Poster Style Map

Luc Messmer

This page describes a map style inspired by posters produced by the Works Progress Administration.

In the 1930s and 1940s the Federal Art Project, under the Works Progress Administration, produced thousands of posters for Federally funded programs such as theater, public awareness campaigns, and the National Parks.

This map style uses subdued colors and a sans serif font to emulate the style of the WPA posters. It favors browns, greens, and blues, with red used to emphasize roadways.
